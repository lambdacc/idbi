# IDBI Hackathon – Research & Implementation Notes

## Overall Understanding

The orientation emphasized that **GST, UPI, EPFO, bank transactions, etc. are only the starting point**. The evaluation is likely to reward teams that can identify and justify additional **electronic data sources** that enrich business profiling, fraud detection, and credit/risk assessment.

The challenge is not to name as many datasets as possible, but to demonstrate:

* Awareness of the broader digital footprint of businesses.
* Understanding of what data actually exists electronically.
* Practicality of obtaining or integrating that data.
* Ability to synthesize multiple data sources into stronger signals.

---

# Research Notes

## Research Objective

Research electronic data sources beyond the obvious datasets.

For every candidate source, determine:

* Does it exist electronically?
* Who owns or generates it?
* Is it realistically accessible?
* How frequently is it updated?
* What business or risk signals does it provide?
* Is it already used by banks, regulators, insurers, or government agencies?
* Can it realistically be incorporated into a production solution?

Avoid "interesting but impractical" ideas.

---

# Implementation Notes

The implementation should **not** treat every dataset independently.

The value comes from **combining multiple electronic sources** to produce richer business intelligence.

The focus should be on feature engineering and signal synthesis.

---

# Implementation Tasks

## Data Source Layer

Create a catalog of all supported data sources.

Each source should define:

* Owner
* Data type
* Refresh frequency
* Reliability
* Availability
* Integration method

---

## Feature Engineering

Identify features that can be derived from individual datasets.

Examples:

* Payment regularity
* Consumption trends
* Seasonal variation
* Workforce stability
* Logistics activity
* Infrastructure utilization
* Digital presence
* Regulatory compliance

---

## Cross-Source Feature Synthesis

Design composite features using multiple datasets.

Potential examples:

* Business Activity Score
* Infrastructure Utilization Index
* Operational Stability Score
* Logistics Consistency Score
* Premises Authenticity Score
* Business Continuity Score
* Compliance Health Score
* Digital Trust Score

These composite signals are likely to provide more predictive value than any single dataset alone.

---

## Prioritization

Prioritize sources that are:

* Electronically available today
* Realistically obtainable
* Frequently updated
* Difficult to manipulate
* Strong predictors of business activity
* Useful across multiple industries

---

# Note to Self

Don't optimize for the **number of data sources**.

Optimize for:

* Practicality
* Electronic availability
* Justified inclusion
* Strong research backing
* Meaningful feature engineering
* Intelligent synthesis across multiple datasets

A small set of well-justified, complementary data sources that produce robust composite indicators is likely to be more compelling than a long list of disconnected datasets.
