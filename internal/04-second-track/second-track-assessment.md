## 1. Executive summary

**Recommendation: add PS4 (Default Prediction) as the second track**, built as a "Track 04 — Portfolio Early Warning" navigation group inside the existing CreditPulse app. It is the only option with ~9/10 reuse of the PS3 build (~2.5–3.5 agent-days incremental), it completes a judge-legible platform story (*underwrite the credit-invisible → monitor the book*), and the research shows early-warning is precisely where the global evidence is strongest and India is weakest: DBS publicly flags **95% of SME NPLs ≥3 months early and averts 80% of at-risk borrowers** — the best-documented AI ROI in this entire scan — while **no Indian bank has an equivalent MSME EWS** and IDBI's own incumbent (SAJAG, per its FY25 annual report) is the "~3-month, low-accuracy internal model" PS4 explicitly asks to beat. We do not compete on synthetic-data AUC; we compete on **12-month lead-time via continuously-refreshed alt-data** (monthly GST, daily UPI, monthly EPFO — the same 25-source footprint PS3 already ingests) plus explainability that RBI's FREE-AI framework (Aug 2025) now effectively mandates.

**PS5 is the strong runner-up, not the winner.** The research confirms high-momentum PS5-eligible waves (agentic fraud operations, agentic credit workflow, compliance GenAI), and §8.4 carries a fully-worked winner concept — an **explainable mule-account detection + agentic case-builder** module riding the MuleHunter.AI/MHA-mandate wave — but every PS5 concept costs ~3+ net-new build-days for less reuse, a smaller standalone prize, and noisier judging. With 5 days to the 9-Jul deadline and PS3 packaging still owed, PS4 dominates on risk-adjusted return. PS5 remains a deliberate option for stage 2 (22–31 Jul) if we are shortlisted and capacity allows.

PS1 (avatar wealth UX on a Streamlit stack, ~2/10 reuse) and PS2 (commoditized retail propensity scoring) are dominated on every axis and rejected.

## 2. Repository findings — what exists and what is reusable

### 2.1 What PS3 is and why it was selected

**CreditPulse — an explainable MSME Financial Health Card** for PS3 ("Financial Health Score"). It scores a credit-invisible (NTC/NTB) MSME's financial health from its **alternate-data footprint** — 25 synthetic sources (GST, bank/UPI cash-flow, EPFO, e-way bills, electricity, licences, court/insolvency, procurement…) — into five dimension scores, a composite 1-10 grade, a lending recommendation with indicative limit, plain-language reason codes, and a flagship **Turnover-Authenticity / fraud-risk score** that cross-checks declared GST turnover against settled bank inflows and physical goods movement.

PS3 was selected (screening 26 Jun, validated post-orientation 28 Jun, founder GO 01 Jul) because it is the one track where Lambdac's moat — Indian MSME/GST data depth + explainable, auditable AI + production-grade engineering — directly produces a better answer than generalist teams, and because the **synthetic-data ceiling** (no real defaults to validate against) punishes accuracy-contest tracks but not a transparency/integration/coverage play.

### 2.2 Existing architecture (all reusable platform surface)

One repo, one container, one deploy (Cloud Run-ready; AWS-portable):

| `app/ml/` | Framework-clean pandas/sklearn/LightGBM: WOE/IV scorecard, monotonic GBM, **out-of-fold probability calibration**, K-Means peer segmentation + PCA projection, **Isolation Forest anomaly leg**, composite indicators, SHAP + native reason codes, prefit/pickle warm-start with version guard, full eval harness (AUC/Gini/KS/PSI/Brier/ECE). ### 2.3 Reusable assets for ANY second track

1. **The platform shell** — multipage Streamlit app + nav, design system, Simple/Technical dual-audience pattern, glossary, view-mode session contract. A second track is literally "add a nav group + pages".
2. **The staged-pipeline orchestrator pattern** — any assessment-shaped workflow (score a lead, monitor a loan, draft a memo) can be decomposed into explainable stages and rendered by the existing pipeline UI for free.
3. **The synthetic-data engine** — latent-driven, cross-source-consistent cohort generation is the hardest asset to rebuild; it extends naturally (e.g., add repayment histories to existing MSMEs, or a retail-customer cohort).
4. **The ML kit** — scorecard/GBM/calibration/clustering/anomaly/eval-harness modules are domain-agnostic; the engine's GBM already outputs **calibrated PD** (a default-prediction primitive).
5. **Explainability-by-construction** — reason codes, SHAP cross-check, technique disclosure cards, audit posture. This is the brand across any track.
6. **Credibility artifacts** — eval discipline, criteria mapping, impact-model template, deployment runbook, honest synthetic-data framing.

### 2.4 Existing assumptions about IDBI Bank

- The bank rewards **deployable, explainable, compliance-aware, measurable-in-banking-terms** builds over flashy demos ("common mistakes" list names theoretical ideas, weak domain understanding, ignoring compliance, incomplete architecture).
- AI/ML adoption in IDBI lending is **currently limited and being scaled up**; they want co-creation and a long-term association; AWS is the cloud/knowledge partner; ACC provides the stage-2 sandbox.
- All data is **mock/synthetic at every stage**; stage-1 (9 Jul) runs on the team's own synthetic data; sandbox + bank-scoped mock data only post-shortlist (21 Jul).
- Judging: **Innovation · Feasibility · Scalability · Business impact · Technical implementation** (weights unpublished).
- PS5 entries are reviewed by **IDBI senior management** and must be **unrelated to PS1–4**; the PS5 prize is standalone (reported ~₹1L, smaller than track prizes) but carries the same PoC-pathway upside.

### 3.3 PS4 — Default Prediction (12-month early warning on existing MSME loans)

The screening verdict "avoid PS4" was made under the **one-track rule**, when PS4 was an *alternative* to PS3 and the synthetic-accuracy ceiling made its core ask unprovable. As an **additional** track the calculus inverts: the expensive parts are already built, and the two tracks compose into one platform story — *underwrite the credit-invisible with the Health Card (PS3), then monitor the book with the Early-Warning System (PS4)*. Onboarding → monitoring is the full MSME credit lifecycle in one app.

| Dimension | Score | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Business value | 9 | The bank *named* its pain: internal model sees only ~3 months ahead with low accuracy; 12-month early warning on the existing MSME book directly attacks NPA cost. |
| Innovation potential | 7 | Not the model — the **explainable EWS workflow**: lead-time analysis, driver decomposition (GST decline, cash-flow stress, utilization drift), watchlist actions, alt-data-refreshed monitoring (the same AA/GST rails as PS3, reused for surveillance). |
| Demo potential | 8 | A portfolio watchlist that *shows* a healthy-looking loan deteriorating 10 months before default — with reasons — is a strong live demo, and it chains naturally after the Health Card demo. |
| Judge appeal | 7 | Same MSME-credit judges as PS3 see a coherent lifecycle platform; risk: they explicitly frame PS4 as an accuracy contest (see risks). |
| Technical complexity | 4 (low-med) | Extend the synthetic engine with loan+repayment histories driven by the *existing* latent health variable; train a horizon model with the existing GBM/calibration/eval kit; add a Portfolio/EWS nav group rendered by existing components. |
| PS3 reuse | **9/10** | Highest possible: same segment, same data instincts, same ML stack (engine already outputs calibrated PD), same explainability, same UI patterns. Genuinely incremental. |
| Timeline fit | 8 | ~2–3 agent-days of incremental build; leaves slack for PS3 polish and submission packaging. |

**Strengths:** maximum reuse, coherent one-platform narrative, named bank pain, cheap. **Weaknesses / risks:** (a) it *is* pitched by the bank as beat-our-model accuracy — on synthetic data we must reframe honestly to lead-time + explainability + workflow, and some judges may not accept the reframe; (b) Prashant verbally blurred PS3/PS4 language — we must draw the boundary crisply (new-borrower assessment vs existing-book surveillance) so the two submissions don't read as one model twice; (c) a crowded track (many ML teams will pick it).

## 4. Phase 2 — Global banking AI landscape (last 12–18 months)

Synthesized from four parallel evidence-graded research streams (US/EU majors; APAC + India + IDBI; consulting/analyst; lending/risk/ops domain). Evidence bar: production deployments, official announcements, earnings calls, named case studies — no "AI could transform banking" pieces. Inline links are the primary sources; the fuller list is in §9.

**The macro picture.** AI in banking crossed from pilot budget to core investment line in 2025: JPMorgan runs 450+ AI use cases against an ~$18B tech budget and projects [$1.5–2B in annual AI value](https://www.constellationr.com/insights/news/jpmorgan-chases-it-ai-bets-where-returns-are); [Gartner forecasts banking IT spend up 9.5% in 2026 to $857.5B](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-02-03-gartner-forecasts-worldwide-it-spending-to-grow-10-point-8-percent-in-2026-totaling-6-point-15-trillion-dollars); [NVIDIA's 2026 FS survey](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/ai-in-financial-services-survey-2026/) has 89% of institutions reporting AI raised revenue and cut costs; McKinsey sizes GenAI at [$200–340B/yr for banking](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/financial-services/our-insights/global-banking-annual-review) and [BCG at $370B additional retail-bank profit by 2030](https://www.bcg.com/press/5november2025-300-billion-profit-potential-retail-banks-via-ai). Banks now disclose AI value in earnings materials (DBS ~SG$1B in 2025; Lloyds ~£50M delivered, £100M+ targeted; Santander €35M in Q1-26, €1B+ through 2028). AI headcount at the 50 biggest banks grew [25% YoY to ~90,000](https://evidentinsights.com/bankingbrief/heres-the-2025-evident-ai-index/).

**The architectural convergence that matters for us:** regulators' data ([ECB supervision newsletter, Nov 2025](https://www.bankingsupervision.europa.eu/press/supervisory-newsletters/newsletter/2025/html/ssm.nl251120_1.en.html)) confirms credit scoring and fraud decisioning still run on **traditional ML** (gradient boosting, trees); GenAI replaced *humans and rules* (drafting, summarizing, chatbot NLU), not the ML decisioning core. The emerging 2026 pattern is **LLM-orchestrated workflows that call traditional ML models as tools, with humans approving** — agents propose, humans decide, every claim cited. That is architecturally identical to CreditPulse's deterministic-first / narrate-on-top design, which means our stack is already shaped like the industry's target state.

**India-specific:** the state supplies the AI rails — [ULI at 64 lenders / 136 data services](https://www.medianama.com/2026/01/223-unified-lending-interface-64-lenders-136-data-services/), [AA at 45 crore consents / ₹1.6 lakh crore FY25 loans](https://sahamati.org.in/sahamati-marks-4th-account-aggregator-foundation-day-indias-data-empowerment-revolution-scales-new-heights/), OCEN 4.0 sachet loans, [MuleHunter.AI live in 23–26 banks](https://www.medianama.com/2025/12/223-rti-23-banks-mulehunter-mule-accounts/) with an MHA directive that **all FIs integrate mule detection by Dec 2026**, and [RBI's FREE-AI framework (Aug 2025)](https://rbidocs.rbi.org.in/rdocs/PublicationReport/Pdfs/FREEAIR130820250A24FF2D4578453F824C72ED9F5D5851.PDF) whose 7 Sutras make **explainability, accountability and fairness** the compliance perimeter for every bank AI deployment. MSME cash-flow lending on GST+AA rails is India's dominant AI-credit use case (SBI MSME Sahaj 15-minute STP loans; IndusInd INDIE for Business; SIDBI GST Sahay) — validating PS3's premise commercially.

**IDBI Bank specifically** (primary-source profile): its deployed AI is **risk-side ML, not GenAI** — SAJAG (predictive early-warning asset monitoring) and an EFRMS fraud platform upgraded with explainable AI and behavioural biometrics (FY25 annual report); its flagship digital push is **cash-flow MSME lending** (i-MSME Express ₹1–25L GST-based ~30-min loans; GeM Sahay 2.0 on OCEN — Finnovity 2025 recognition; co-lending ₹250cr→₹900cr in a year). CDO Binit Jha frames AI holistically across "predictive analytics, fraud detection, cybersecurity and CX"; there is **no public IDBI GenAI product as of Jul 2026** — it sits behind SBI ([YONO Ji agentic assistant, Jul 2026](https://www.business-standard.com/amp/companies/news/sbi-rolls-out-ai-powered-features-on-yono-expands-trade-finance-services-126070101278_1.html)) and BoB (Aditi GenAI virtual RM) among PSU-lineage peers, and is explicitly using this hackathon to source AI capability. Privatisation context: Emirates NBD (itself a GenAI-heavy bank) is a live bidder for the government stake.

## 6. Deliverable — Industry trends

**Commoditized (table stakes by mid-2026):** customer chatbots (~98% of retail banks); internal GenAI assistants/portals (every top-20 US/EU bank); developer copilots (~80% adoption); document summarization/call summarization (Barclays: 8M+ calls); KYC OCR/IDP; fraud-scoring ML per se (9 in 10 banks). *A hackathon demo of any of these, as such, is a losing entry.*

**Saturated but still yielding:** knowledge-RAG assistants, advisor meeting copilots, contact-center assist, marketing content GenAI.

**Emerging / differentiating (where the 2025–26 announcements cluster):**
- **Agentic workflows in regulated processes** — credit memos (Moody's/S&P/nCino), fraud-rule authorship (CommBank), parallel investigation agents (Lloyds Envoy), KYC refresh (BCG's "agentic KYC revolution": −90% onboarding time). BCG counts **160+ agentic use cases announced by top-50 banks in 2025**; McKinsey: 62% of orgs experimenting with agents but ≤10% scaled in any function — early, hence differentiating.
- **Continuous-signal risk monitoring** (EWS) — proven at DBS, sold B2B by OakNorth, absent in India.
- **AI governance as a product feature** — 42 of 50 Evident banks have senior responsible-AI leads; ECB/RBI both signal explainability-first supervision. FREE-AI makes this a *scoring criterion* in India.
- **Vernacular voice in the credit lifecycle** (India) — RBI×Bhashini MoU (Feb 2026) for 22-language banking AI; Sarvam-powered collections at Tata Capital; voice UPI live.

**Where GenAI replaces vs augments traditional ML:** GenAI replaced rules/NLU chatbots, manual drafting, and document handling. It did **not** replace ML decisioning — credit scoring and fraud interception remain gradient-boosted/logistic per ECB supervision data. The winning 2026 architecture is **agentic orchestration over deterministic ML tools with human approval and citation trails** — i.e., GenAI narrates and coordinates; ML decides; humans own the decision. (This is CreditPulse's existing architecture, independently validated.)

**Where agentic AI is appearing:** three insertion points in lending/risk — credit-memo drafting (most mature), fraud-defence authorship, trade-doc examination (PoC stage). Common design everywhere: *agents propose, humans approve, everything cited*.

### 8.2 Why PS4 wins (reasoning)

1. **The research's strongest ROI evidence is exactly this use case.** DBS's EWS (flag 95% of SME NPLs ≥3 months ahead; avert 80% of at-risk borrowers) is the best-quantified bank-AI deployment found anywhere in the 4-stream scan; OakNorth proves EWS engines are so valuable banks *buy them from other lenders*. India-side, the scan found **no deployed Indian MSME EWS equivalent** — a real white space.
2. **IDBI named its own incumbent.** PS4's "internal model, ~3 months, low accuracy" matches SAJAG (FY25 annual report). We are not guessing at the pain; we're answering a documented gap, and our pitch line writes itself: *"SAJAG sees 3 months ahead from repayment behaviour; CreditPulse EWS sees 12 months ahead because the alt-data footprint (monthly GST, daily UPI, monthly EPFO) deteriorates long before an EMI bounces."*
3. **Maximum reuse, minimum risk.** The engine already computes calibrated PD; the synthetic cohort already carries latent health trajectories; the orchestrator/pipeline/explainability/UI patterns all transfer. No new dependencies, no runtime LLM, no new deployment.
4. **One coherent platform beats two clever demos.** PS3+PS4 in one app is the MSME credit lifecycle — *onboard the credit-invisible, then monitor the book* — which converts the multi-track allowance into a strategic statement: CreditPulse is a platform, and Lambdac is a partner, not a point-solution vendor. Same judges, compounding credibility.
5. **The honesty play transfers.** We reframe PS4 from "beat our AUC" (unprovable on synthetic data — the same trap that made us avoid PS4 as a *primary* track) to **lead-time + capture-rate + explainable drivers + intervention workflow**, with real-default recalibration stated as the pilot step. FREE-AI's explainability sutras make this framing a scoring advantage, not a dodge.

**Risks & mitigations:** (a) *accuracy-contest judges* — lead with lead-time metrics and DBS benchmark framing; show the eval harness so rigor is visible. (b) *PS3/PS4 blur* (Prashant verbally called PS4 a "financial health scorecard") — draw the boundary on-screen: Track 03 = new-borrower assessment, Track 04 = existing-book surveillance; different pages, different data (loan+repayment histories), same platform. (c) *timeline* — the cut-line order below protects the 9-Jul submission.

### 8.3 PS4 build & demo plan (single-app, maximal reuse)

**Navigation:** existing pages regroup under **"Track 03 — Financial Health Score"**; new group **"Track 04 — Portfolio Early Warning"** with 2 pages (+1 stretch). One codebase, one deploy, shared sidebar brand, shared Simple/Technical toggle.

**Incremental build (≈2.5–3.5 agent-days):**
1. `data_gen/generators/loan_book.py` (+0.5–1d): for ~60% of the existing cohort, synthesize a sanctioned MSME loan (limit from PS3 grade, vintage, EMI schedule) + 24 months of repayment/utilization history driven by the *existing* `true_health` latent with deterioration arcs (GST turnover decline → UPI inflow drop → EPFO headcount cut → utilization creep → first EMI delay → default). Defaults visible in alt-data 6–12 months before the event *by construction* — same latent-consistency trick PS3 already uses.
2. `ml/ews.py` (+0.5–1d): 12-month-horizon PD from monthly snapshots — behavioural features (DPD, utilization drift, bounce count) + alt-data deltas (filing gaps, inflow decline, payroll shrink). Reuse GBM + calibration + eval harness; add EWS metrics: **lead-time distribution, capture@top-decile, alert precision**; Green/Amber/Red bands + reason codes via the existing explainability kit. Include a deliberately handicapped "repayment-only, 3-month" baseline model — the SAJAG stand-in — for the on-screen comparison.
3. `backend/services/ews_orchestrator.py` (+0.5d): monthly monitoring run decomposed into explainable stages (refresh alt-data → recompute health → horizon PD → band migration → watchlist actions), reusing the Stage/findings/technique-disclosure machinery.
4. Frontend Track-04 pages (+0.5–1d): **Portfolio Overview** (book health grid, band-migration flow, capture/lead-time KPIs) and **Watchlist & Borrower Drilldown** (ranked alerts; per-borrower timeline showing alt-data signals deteriorating months before the repayment signal; side-by-side "3-month repayment-only model vs 12-month alt-data model" first-alert markers; recommended action per RBI EWS red-flag conventions). Reuse charts/kpi/stage components; both view modes; jargon guardrail extended.
5. Tests + demo-script section (+0.5d): generator schema tests, EWS metric tests, AppTest smoke for the new pages in both modes.

**Demo scenario (90s add-on after the PS3 demo):** open Portfolio Overview → "of 240 live MSME loans, 12 flagged Red" → click the flagship deteriorating borrower → timeline shows GST filings slipping and UPI inflows sagging 10 months before the first EMI miss, while the repayment-only baseline stays green until month −2 → reason codes in plain language → recommended action (limit review + restructure offer) → close: "same data rails, same explainability, now protecting the book — SAJAG sees 3 months; this sees 12, and every alert says why."

**Cut lines if time runs short:** stretch page (EWS pipeline animation) → drop; band-migration chart → drop; the two core pages + metrics + drilldown timeline are the must-demo MVP.

### 8.4 PS5 winner concept (runner-up — hold for stage 2): "SentinelPulse" — explainable mule-account detection + agentic case-builder

The best PS5-eligible concept from the research, fully worked so it can be picked up cold:

- **Problem statement:** Mule accounts are the plumbing of India's digital-fraud epidemic. RBI/RBIH shipped MuleHunter.AI (network-level, now in 23–26 banks) and **MHA has directed every FI to integrate mule detection by Dec 2026** — but bank-side fraud teams still triage alerts manually and assemble STR/case files by hand, and FREE-AI requires every AI flag to be explainable. The gap is the **bank-side layer**: explainable triage + investigation + case-file automation on top of network-level detection.
- **Target users:** IDBI fraud-ops/AML analysts and the EFRMS team.
- **Concept:** extend the synthetic engine with transaction-graph patterns (the 19 public mule typologies: fan-in/fan-out, dormancy-burst, rapid pass-through, KYC-profile mismatch) → detect with an ensemble reusing our Isolation-Forest + calibration stack + typology rules → **explainable flag** (which typology, which transactions, plain language) → an **agentic multi-stage investigation pipeline** (evidence gathering → counterparty tracing → typology confirmation → draft case file/STR with every claim citing specific transactions), rendered by the existing staged-pipeline UI, human-approves-at-the-end. Deterministic-first, citation-gated — no runtime LLM required, honestly framed as "agentic workflow, human decision".
- **Single-app fit:** "Track 05 — Fraud Intelligence" nav group; reuses data-gen engine, anomaly/calibration/eval ML kit, orchestrator stages, pipeline animation, explainability components, dual view modes.
- **Reuse ≈ 6/10; effort ≈ 3–3.5 net-new agent-days; innovation 9; judge appeal 8–9** (senior-management audience; regulatory-mandate hook; "not a chatbot" by construction).
- **Risks:** duplicates the *purpose* of IDBI's internal EFRMS (position as its explainable next generation); PS5's smaller standalone prize; three tracks in 5 days risks degrading all three — hence **stage-2 hold**.
- **Why it beats generic chatbot demos:** it demonstrates the industry's actual 2026 frontier (agents that investigate and draft, humans that decide — the CommBank/Lloyds/Moody's pattern) on a problem with a statutory deadline, with explainability as the differentiator FREE-AI demands.
